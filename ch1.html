Three misconceptions about Computer Science:
  1. Computer science is the study of computers.
  2. Computer science is the study of how to write computer programs.
  3. Computer science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software.

Briefly define:
  1. Computer Science:
    Computer science is the study of algorithms, including their amthematical properties hardware and linguistic realizations, and their applications.
  2. Algorithm:
    An ordered sequence of instructions that is guaranteed to solve a problem.
  3. Sequential Operation:
    Algorithmic structure that carries out a single task and then moves on to the next operations in sequence.
  4. Conditional Operation:
    Algorithmic operation that asks a question and selects the next step to carry out based on the answer to the question.
  5. Iterative Operation:
    Algorithmic operation that repeats a block of instructions.
  6. Stored Progam Computer:
    A model of computation in which the instructions to be executed are represented as binary string and stored in the memory of the computer.
  7. Programmer:
    Someone who is able to write commands in a high-level language.
  8. Integrated Circuit:
    Used to reducd the cost of computers, integrated circuits were used rather than discrete electronic components. Integrated circuits along with transistors, resistors, and capacitors were photographically on to a piece of silicon.

Briefly describe these computer generations:
  1. First Generation Computer:
    Computers built from aproxamitly 1950 to 1957 were called the first genersation computers. Computers such as UNIVAC I and the IBM 701
  2. Second Generation Computer:
    From roughly around 1957 to 1965, major changes to size and complexity of computers making them more reliable. At this time computers were made availble primarly for business owners, colleges, and government agencies.
  3. Third Generation Computer:
    This generation lasted from around 1965 to 1975. This began with the integratd circuit era and was also the birth of the sofaware industry. Here is when the first minicomputer was created.
  4. Fourth Generation Computer:
    Lasting from 1975 to 1985 was the fourth generation of computers. This was also the first appreance of the microcomputer. A complete computer system could now be contained on a single circuit board. This era also began the first appearence of computer networks.
  5. Fifth Generation Computer:
    Fifth generation is from 1985 till today. Ultra-large-scale integrated circutis, supercmoputers and parallel processer. This was also the start of laptops, smartpohnes and tablets and much of the technology you see today.
